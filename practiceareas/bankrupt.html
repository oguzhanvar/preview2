Bankruptcy is a legal process designed to provide individuals and businesses overwhelmed by debt with a fresh financial start. It offers various solutions tailored to different circumstances. Chapter 7 bankruptcy, often referred to as liquidation bankruptcy, involves the sale of non-exempt assets to discharge most unsecured debts. This process typically takes a few months and is suitable for individuals with limited income and significant debt. Chapter 13 bankruptcy, on the other hand, enables individuals to reorganize their debts into manageable repayment plans spanning three to five years. This option is ideal for those with a steady income who want to keep their assets while repaying creditors over time. Additionally, Chapter 11 bankruptcy primarily serves businesses by allowing them to restructure their operations and debts under court supervision, enabling them to continue operating and eventually regain financial stability.

The bankruptcy process involves several key steps to help individuals and businesses resolve their financial difficulties. It begins with the filing of a bankruptcy petition, along with a detailed inventory of assets, debts , income, and expenses. An automatic stay is then issued, halting all collection efforts and providing relief from creditor harassment. A bankruptcy trustee is appointed to oversee the case and assess the assets. For Chapter 7, non-exempt assets are sold, and the proceeds are distributed to creditors. In Chapter 13, a repayment plan is proposed based on the debtor's disposable income, which, once approved by the court, allows the debtor to make monthly payments to the trustee. Successful completion of the plan results in a discharge of remaining eligible debts. Throughout the process, legal counsel plays a crucial role in guiding clients, ensuring proper documentation, and advocating on their behalf in court.
